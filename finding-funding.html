Fund Your Startup Dream

Discover comprehensive resources for finding government and non-government funding for your startup. From grants to venture capital, we'll guide you through the maze of financial opportunities to fuel your innovation.Starting a business can be challenging, but finding the right funding shouldn't be. Whether you're looking for government grants or private investment, we provide a comprehensive guide to help you navigate the financial landscape and fuel your innovation.

Government Funding for Startups

                1. Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)
                   Provides up to ₹50 lakhs for proof of concept, 
                   prototype development, and commercialization. 
                   Open to DPIIT-recognized startups less than 2 years old.
                
                2. Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS)
                   A corpus of ₹10,000 crores managed by SIDBI to 
                   support startups through Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs).
                
                3. Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE)
                   Offers up to ₹2 crores as credit guarantee to
                    financial institutions lending to startups and SMEs.
                
                4. Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
                   Provides loans up to ₹10 lakhs to non-corporate,
                   non-farm small/micro enterprises.
                
                5. Atal Innovation Mission (AIM)
                   Supports innovation and entrepreneurship with programs like 
                   Atal Incubation Centres (AICs) and Atal Tinkering Labs (ATLs).

Non-Government Funding Options

                1. Angel Investors
                   Individuals providing early-stage capital in 
                   exchange for equity or convertible debt.
                   Funding typically ranges from ₹5 lakhs to ₹2 crores.
                
                2. Venture Capital (VC) Firms
                   Investment firms funding high-growth startups 
                   in exchange for equity.They usually invest in later 
                   stages with funding ranging from ₹2 crores to ₹100 crores or more.
                
                3. Crowdfunding Platforms
                   Websites that enable startups to raise small amounts 
                   from many people. Popular platforms include Kickstarter, Indiegogo,
                   and Ketto. Funding can range from a few thousand to several lakhs.
                
                4. Startup Incubators
                   Programs offering mentorship, office space, and sometimes funding.
                   Examples include Y Combinator, Techstars, T-Hub, and SINE.
                   Funding ranges from ₹5 lakhs to ₹50 lakhs.
                
                5. Startup Accelerators
                  Programs providing mentorship, office space, and seed funding
                  in exchange for equity, culminating in a demo day.
                  Examples include 500 Startups, GSF Accelerator, and Axilor Ventures.
                  Funding typically ranges from ₹10 lakhs to ₹1 crore.
